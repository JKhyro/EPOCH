# WORKSHOP Extraction Boundary

## Purpose

This document records which historical EPOCH prototype surfaces should move to
WORKSHOP or become WORKSHOP-owned integrations.

EPOCH should keep schedule-bound primitives. WORKSHOP should own revenue
production.

## Move to WORKSHOP ownership

- education service catalog and offer packaging
- EIKEN/test-prep positioning and package strategy
- async correction packs, cohorts, subscription materials, and study strategy
  products
- consulting, tech-support, clerical/admin, database, CRM, management-system,
  and AI/dev service lanes
- marketing and advertising plans
- lead conversion and revenue pipeline strategy
- ARA income-stream production and service-delivery automation
- pricing, eligibility, under-19 acceptance policy, and sales copy

## Keep in EPOCH ownership

- sessions, appointments, and calendar events
- availability windows
- reminder and recurrence candidates
- submission windows and review deadlines
- follow-up checkpoints
- customer-safe schedule and deadline status
- monitor health for schedule-bound work

## Integration model

WORKSHOP may call EPOCH for scheduling and status. EPOCH should not embed
WORKSHOP's business model as its own core scope.

The correct relationship is:

1. WORKSHOP creates a service, request, cohort, submission, or delivery plan.
2. EPOCH schedules the time-bound pieces.
3. EPOCH returns schedule/status events.
4. WORKSHOP owns revenue, delivery, marketing, and customer/account strategy.

## Current prototype handling

The existing EPOCH static web prototype contains commercial records. Do not
treat that as final product ownership. Until those records are physically moved,
they are extraction candidates and compatibility scaffolding only.
